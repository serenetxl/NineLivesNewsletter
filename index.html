The Stories That Defined This Tournament

🎰
The Finals Changed Everything
With a ×10 payout, the Finals reshaped the entire leaderboard in one match. Four players correctly called 1:Huangjii · 2:Sanhua · 3:OPM and walked away with payouts between 50,000 and 105,000 CC each. Chion and Dumamay each turned 10,500 CC into 105,000 CC. Meanwhile AniNing staked 41,000 CC on the wrong order and lost every single CC of it.
🔥
The Great WC3 Massacre
25 people bet a combined 204,482 CC on tipsyneko in WC3. Sanhua won. Every single one of them lost — including tipsyneko themselves. Biggest victims: xZel (−38,800), Mojo (−36,000), Dumamay (−13,150). The most expensive collective misread of the tournament.
🔮
iLYa: The Untouchable Oracle
Best net profit: +136,630 CC. Went 5/5 in semis and finals phase, 12W/7L overall. Bet 23,000 on Sanhua in WC3 while 25 others were backing tipsyneko. Also placed top 3 in the Finals. The most CC won, the clearest read on every match, start to finish.
😸
Chion's Redemption Arc
7 consecutive losses. A trough of −6,440 CC. A 47.6% overall win rate. Then: 10,500 CC on the correct Finals order → 105,000 CC payout. Chion ends the tournament with the 4th best net profit at +98,560 CC. The longest cold streak ended with the biggest single-bet return.
📉
Mojo's Complicated Legacy
Most CC wagered: 167,858 CC. Hit a peak of +108,960 CC, then lost nearly 57,000 CC across three catastrophic bets. But with all ×2 payouts properly counted, Mojo still finishes net positive at +52,072 CC — the high roller who survived their own worst impulses, barely.
🎯
FortunaKirana: The Quiet Assassin
Never splashed huge amounts. Just went 15W / 4L (79%) — best win rate among anyone with 10+ bets. Net profit of +6,900 CC. The sharpest read on match outcomes, every round, without a word of drama about it.
💀
The 12 Truly Indebted
At tournament's end, 12 members have negative net worth — their debt to the Cat Bank exceeds their remaining balance. Deepest in the hole: AniNing, Gunbae, and xZel (all at −22,920 CC net worth). The Cat Bank has never been busier.
